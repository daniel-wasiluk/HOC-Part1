Steve Wozniak - Awards

National Medal of Technology and Innovation (1985)

This prestigious award was presented to Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs by then-President Ronald Reagan. It recognized their development and introduction of the personal computer, which ignited the birth of a new industry and extended the power of computing to individual users. This award underscores Wozniak's critical role in pioneering personal computing. (Source)

Heinz Award in Technology, the Economy, and Employment (2001)

Wozniak received the 7th Heinz Award for his groundbreaking design of the first personal computer and for redirecting his lifelong passion for mathematics and electronics towards igniting excitement for education among grade school students and their teachers. The award celebrates Wozniak's innovations in technology as well as his commitment to education and philanthropy, emphasizing his belief in making learning accessible and engaging for young students. (Source)

Induction into the Inventors Hall of Fame (2000)

Wozniak's induction into the Inventors Hall of Fame further acknowledges his significant contributions to the field of technology, specifically for his work in co-creating the Apple I and Apple II computers. These inventions not only helped launch the personal computer revolution but also made computing accessible to a broader audience, solidifying Wozniak's legacy as a key figure in the history of technology. (Source)
